This proposal outlines a functionality-based pricing model for website design and development services. Rather than pricing by the number of pages, this approach prices each discrete feature or module independently — ensuring fair, transparent, and scalable costs regardless of how complex a single page becomes. Clients select only the features they need, making budgeting predictable for both parties.

⑥ Important Notes & Best Practices
01
Scope Discovery First
Always conduct a requirements session before issuing a quote. Use a checklist of all functionalities and let clients confirm what they need.
02
Define "Done" Clearly
Document exactly what each feature includes. Avoid scope creep by attaching feature specifications to the signed agreement.
03
Set Revision Limits
2 rounds of design revisions are included in the base. One round = one consolidated feedback submission covering any number of comments on the current design. Additional rounds are charged at RM 200 per round.
04
Bundle for Clarity
Consider offering preset bundles (Starter, Business, Enterprise) built from the modules above for clients who are unsure where to start.
05
Third-Party Costs
Prices above cover integration work only. Third-party licensing fees (hosting, SMS, payment gateway, SaaS tools) are billed separately to the client.
06
Technology Flexibility
The stack used (WordPress, React, Laravel, etc.) may affect pricing. Specific technology choices will be discussed and agreed upon during scoping.
